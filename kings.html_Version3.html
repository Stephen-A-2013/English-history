Kings and Queens of England Since 1066

Page 1: Early Monarchs (1066 - 1485)

William the Conqueror (1066 - 1087)

William I, also known as William the Conqueror, was the first Norman King of England. He defeated Harold II at the Battle of Hastings in 1066 and established Norman rule over England.

Henry II (1154 - 1189)

Henry II founded the Plantagenet dynasty. His reign was marked by legal reforms and conflicts with Thomas Becket, Archbishop of Canterbury.

Richard the Lionheart (1189 - 1199)

Richard I is famous for his role in the Crusades and his reputation as a brave warrior. He spent much of his reign abroad.

Page 2: Tudor and Stuart Eras (1485 - 1714)

Henry VIII (1509 - 1547)

Henry VIII is perhaps most famous for his six marriages and for initiating the English Reformation, breaking from the Catholic Church to form the Church of England.

Elizabeth I (1558 - 1603)

Elizabeth I, known as the Virgin Queen, presided over a golden age in English history. Her reign saw the defeat of the Spanish Armada and a flourishing of English culture.

Charles I (1625 - 1649)

Charles I's conflict with Parliament led to the English Civil War. He was eventually tried and executed for treason, leading to a temporary abolition of the monarchy.

Page 3: Georgian to Victorian Era (1714 - 1901)

George III (1760 - 1820)

George III is remembered for losing the American colonies and for his struggles with mental illness later in life.

Queen Victoria (1837 - 1901)

Queen Victoria's reign was the longest of any British monarch until Elizabeth II. The Victorian Era was a period of industrial expansion, empire building, and social reform.

Page 4: Modern Monarchs (1901 - Present)

George VI (1936 - 1952)

George VI led the country through World War II and is remembered for his quiet strength and dedication. He was the father of Queen Elizabeth II.

Elizabeth II (1952 - 2022)

Elizabeth II was the longest-reigning monarch in British history. Her reign saw significant changes in society and the monarchy's role.

Charles III (2022 - Present)

Charles III succeeded his mother, Elizabeth II. He has long supported environmental and charitable causes and continues to define his role as king in a modern age.
